CUSTOMER ACQUISITION STRATEGY

Smooth’s acquisition strategy is tailored to a phased approach to market. The model is fluid, in that it utilises different channels in each stage of the product’s life-cycle.

Phase 1...

  • Traction

    In his book, Traction, Justin Mares suggests that phase 1 (of gaining traction) is very product focused, and involves getting traction in ways that don’t necessarily scale.

  • Collision Installation

    Paul Graham of Y Combinator echoes this sentiment through a strategy adopted by Stripe, which he dubbed “Collision Installation” - Stripe would physically install the app on customers’ laptops.

  • Go to your customer!

    Paul’s advice to Brian Chesky, of Airbnb, was in a similar vein, that he should go to where his early adopters were.

Over the past 4 years, we’ve been developing Smooth within our restaurant, which is part of a particularly strong franchise. Upon demonstrating the capabilities of the product to the franchisor, he actioned an initial roll-out to the franchise manager’s store.

Our strategy during this phase, is to be strongly product-focussed - working closely with the staff in the franchise manager’s store, located in Springs. At this stage, a ‘product-oriented’ acquisition channel is particularly important, given the number of parties at play in a restaurant environment.

Building Smooth from within our own restaurant provided us with a platform to create a product from each staff member’s vantage point.

Alex Christopoulos, co-founder of Smooth Restaurants.

We are Insiders

Strong focus on each individual role in the restaurant

Having worked closely with operators, front-of-house managers, waiters and back-of-house staff, we expect the uptake in other restaurants to be exponentially quicker than other solutions in the market.

During the roll-out to Springs, we’ll be keeping a close eye on users’ engagement with the product, and will be on-hand to ensure the transition is as smooth as possible. We’ll evaluate queries and requests in real-time, and refine the product through these interactions, paving the way for an improved user experience as we roll out to the other stores in the group.

Our relationships with the other franchisees in the group puts us in good stead to really dig into the feedback, and make adjustments where necessary before pushing Smooth to a broader market. During this phase of the acquisition process, we’re placing a strong focus on developing a framework for the learning material we’ll be incorporating in Smooth.

Learning Curve Reduced

One of the barriers to adoption in this space is the learning curve associated with different points of sale.

Restaurateurs don’t have a lot of love for the solutions they currently use, but the thought of implementing a similar point of sale, and having to go through the process of familiarising themselves with something new is a daunting and expensive task.

The training associated with this switch-over can take anywhere from 5-7 days, sometimes even longer, and it costs in the realm of R7500 - R10 000 - reserves a restaurant doesn’t have after an excessively high capital outlay for hardware. We’ve innovated, by an order of magnitude, at every step of the process - providing a shop more than enough reason to make that switch. We would like the process of training and on-boarding to be just as innovative, and another compelling differentiator.

Phase 1 Roll-out

Working closely with a user-base spanning 17 stores will provide a solid means from which to tailor our tutorial videos and have them in the app. This has a significant impact on the customer acquisition strategy implemented moving forward - allowing us to make use of broader channels in subsequent phases.

Smooth will be a complete plug-and-play solution - no on-site training needed - truly a first of its kind. This product-centric phase of acquisition involves roll-out to the following stores...

Phase 2...

Modifications and updates made to Smooth, as a result of our close interaction with the stores from the early roll-out, will set us up for expansive marketing efforts in this phase. At this point, we’ve re-defined the restaurant hardware and software landscape, by driving up the value of the software offering, while simultaneously driving down the hardware and software costs.

The next step is to visit outlying franchise groups already in our pipeline. There are 2 franchises of particular interest, who happen to have a significant footprint in the local space...

Doppio Zero

Having touched base with co-founder Paul Christie a year ago, he put us in contact with the operations manager of the group, Jason Carrozzo, to evaluate Smooth’s compatibility with the restaurant chain’s operations.

Smooth ticked all the boxes, and he committed to a roll-out in the new store they were opening in Kyalami, and a phased approach to the other stores in the group once the team finds its feet in the transition. We were still in the process of building Smooth’s inventory feature at the time, but he committed to on-boarding as soon as that was complete. Needless to say, we’re a lot further down the road now.

There are 18 Doppio Zero stores across the country, and 2 more in Egypt. A local franchise with cross-border roots allows us to establish a presence on the international scene, within a controlled environment.

Piza e Vino

Piza e Vino is a part of the Doppio Zero group. Our conversation with Mr Carrozzo involved a roll-out to all stores under the umbrella after up-take at the Kyalami branch.

This includes 13 Piza e Vino locations nation-wide.

Establishing a presence within these stores is likely to generate substantial market-buzz.

A strong focus on the franchise

  • Address Transparency

    We’ve baked certain features into Smooth that make it incomparable to other solutions from the perspective of the franchisor. Traditional points of sale offer zero transparency and control to the franchisor.

  • Address Topology

    Solutions are disconnected to such an extent, that the franchisor has to rely on franchisees for submission of turnover reports or other data and statistics which can offer significant value in plotting marketing and operational initiatives.

  • Consolidated View

    One of the ways in which Smooth addresses this is through a feature called “Dashboard”. A franchisor now has the ability to get real-time data on any store in the group. Live updates to menus, food cost, turnover, purchases - it’s now in plain sight of the franchisor, and under his complete control. As it should be.

  • Rollout

    After establishing industry credibility through installation in franchises such as Cappuccinos, Doppio Zero and Piza e Vino, our acquisition strategy in phase 2 focuses on a much wider franchise roll-out.

Untapped Opportunity

Pop-up shops and markets.

  • Plug & Play

    Smooth is the only plug-and-play solution in the point of sale space. It’s now possible for someone to download an app on a phone, input menu items and prices, and trade with full reporting and statistics within a matter of minutes.

  • Informal Economy

    Smooth enables us to empower, and at the same time capitalise on players in the informal economy. Any vendor, weekend pop-up shop, or stalls at farmer markets can now be up and running with Smooth on just a mobile device.

  • Daily Rate

    This market is a significant contributor to the country’s economy, and up to this point, completely untapped. We’re able to capture this market through zero hardware cost, and a daily rate that’s affordable for these types of operators.

App Stores

Initial penetration within the app stores will be based on targeted marketing, aiming for smaller groups in international markets to adopt Smooth as their point of sale. A focus on a narrow channel online will provide a means to familiarise ourselves with the risks and operational requirements in overseas markets.

Our intention is to have a support team dedicated to this roll-out - personalising the customer’s experience online, and iterating where necessary to ensure an easy uptake moving forward.

Phase 3...

In this phase, Smooth is deeply integrated with industry processes, delivering more value and opening new revenue streams.

Our focus shifts from early adopters online, to scale customers. Support systems and infrastructure are in place to support this growth, and our efforts will focus on international expansion through the App stores.

Our phased approach to customer acquisition lends itself to an improved product, and a business model designed in a way that easily scales and avoids stall points.

Exit Strategy

Smooth’s significant growth in this space presents a number of viable options in terms of an exit. Our preferred exit strategy following market expansion would be through acquisition from a primary player in this space. Acquisitions are commonplace in the cloud-based point of sale space. Some examples of recent purchases follow below...

  • 6 August 2019

    Jonas Software acquired ITWERCS point of sale, value undisclosed.

  • 8 August 2019

    Paytronix Systems Inc. acquired Open Dining, a provider of ordering and delivery technology for restaurants.

  • 20 October 2019

    Lightspeed POS Inc. acquired Kounta, a rapidly growing Australian-based cloud based platform for $35.3 million, with an additional R3.4 million tied to a marketing alliance agreement.

  • 7 January 2020

    Lightspeed POS Inc. acquired Gastrofix, a German cloud-based POS solution for $61 million.
